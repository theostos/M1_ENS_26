    Left invariant derivations #

    In this file we define the concept of left invariant derivation for a Lie group. The concept is analogous to the more classical concept of left invariant vector fields, and it holds that the derivation associated to a vector field is left invariant iff the field is.

    Moreover we prove that LeftInvariantDerivation I G has the structure of a Lie algebra, hence implementing one of the possible definitions of the Lie algebra attached to a Lie group.

    Note that one can also define a Lie algebra on the space of left-invariant vector fields (see instLieAlgebraGroupLieAlgebra). For finite-dimensional C^âˆ real manifolds, the space of derivations can be canonically identified with the tangent space, and we recover the same Lie algebra structure (TODO: prove this). In other smoothness classes or on other fields, this identification is not always true, though, so the derivations point of view does not work in these settings. The left-invariant vector fields should therefore be favored to construct a theory of Lie groups in suitable generality.
