    Local frames in a vector bundle #

    Let V ‚Üí M be a finite rank smooth vector bundle with standard fiber F. A family of sections s i of V ‚Üí M is called a C^k local frame on a set U ‚äÜ M iff each section s i is C^k on U, and the section values s i x form a basis for each x ‚àà U. We define a predicate IsLocalFrame for a collection of sections to be a local frame on a set, and define basic notions (such as the coefficients of a section w.r.t. a local frame, and checking the smoothness of t via its coefficients in a local frame).

    Given a basis b for F and a local trivialisation e for V, we construct a smooth local frame on V w.r.t. e and b, i.e. a collection of sections s·µ¢ of V which is smooth on e.baseSet such that {s·µ¢ x} is a basis of V x for each x ‚àà e.baseSet. Any section s of e can be uniquely written as s = ‚àë i, f^i s·µ¢ near x, and s is smooth at x iff the functions f^i are.

    In this file, we prove the latter statement for finite-rank bundles (with coefficients in a complete field). In Mathlib/Geometry/Manifold/VectorBundle/OrthonormalFrame.lean (#26221), we will prove the same for real vector bundles of any rank which admit a C^n bundle metric. This includes bundles of finite rank, modelled on a Hilbert space or on a Banach space which has smooth partitions of unity.

    We will use this to construct local extensions of a vector to a section which is smooth on the trivialisation domain.

    Main definitions and results #

    • IsLocalFrameOn: a family of sections s i of V ‚Üí M is called a C^k local frame on a set U ‚äÜ M iff each section s i is C^k on U, and the section values s i x form a basis for each x ‚àà U

    Suppose {s·µ¢} is a local frame on U, and hs : IsLocalFrameOn s U.

    • IsLocalFrameOn.toBasisAt hs: for each x ‚àà U, the vectors s·µ¢ x form a basis of F
    • IsLocalFrameOn.coeff hs describes the coefficient of sections of V w.r.t. {s·µ¢}. hs.coeff i is a linear map from sections of V to functions M ‚Üí ùïú.
    • IsLocalFrameOn.eventually_eq_sum_coeff_smul hs: for a local frame {s·µ¢} near x, for each section t we have t = ‚àë i, (hs.coeff i t) ‚Ä¢ s·µ¢.
    • IsLocalFrameOn.coeff_sum_eq hs t hx proves that t x = ‚àë i, (hs.coeff i t) x ‚Ä¢ s·µ¢ x, provided that hx : x ‚àà U.
    • IsLocalFrameOn.coeff_congr hs: the coefficient hs.coeff i of t in the local frame {s·µ¢} only depends on t at x.
    • IsLocalFrameOn.eq_iff_coeff hs: two sections t and t' are equal at x if and only if their coefficients at x w.r.t. {s·µ¢} agree.
    • IsLocalFrameOn.contMDiffOn_of_coeff hs: a section t is C^k on U if each coefficient hs.coeff i t is C^k on U
    • IsLocalFrameOn.contMDiffAt_of_coeff hs: a section t is C^k at x ‚àà U if all of its frame coefficients are
    • IsLocalFrameOn.contMDiffOn_off_coeff hs: a section t is C^k on an open set t ‚äÜ U ff all of its frame coefficients are

    In the following lemmas, let e be a compatible local trivialisation of V, and b a basis of the model fiber F.

    • Trivialization.basisAt e b: for each x ‚àà e.baseSet, return the basis of V x induced by e and b

    • e.contMDiffOn_localFrame_baseSet: each section e.localFrame b i is smooth on e.baseSet

    Implementation notes #

    Local frames use the junk value pattern: they are defined on all of M, but their value is only meaningful on the set on which they are a local frame.
