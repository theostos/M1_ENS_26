    Adjunctions in bicategories #

    For 1-morphisms f : a ‚ü∂ b and g : b ‚ü∂ a in a bicategory, an adjunction between f and g consists of a pair of 2-morphism Œ∑ : ùüô a ‚ü∂ f ‚â´ g and Œµ : g ‚â´ f ‚ü∂ ùüô b satisfying the triangle identities. The 2-morphism Œ∑ is called the unit and Œµ is called the counit.

    Main definitions #

    • Bicategory.Adjunction: adjunctions between two 1-morphisms.
    • Bicategory.Equivalence: adjoint equivalences between two objects.
    • Bicategory.mkOfAdjointifyCounit: construct an adjoint equivalence from 2-isomorphisms Œ∑ : ùüô a ‚âÖ f ‚â´ g and Œµ : g ‚â´ f ‚âÖ ùüô b, by upgrading Œµ to a counit.
