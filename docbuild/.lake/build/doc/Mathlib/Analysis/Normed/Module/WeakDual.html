    Weak dual of normed space #

    Let E be a normed space over a field ùïú. This file is concerned with properties of the weak-* topology on the dual of E. By the dual, we mean either of the type synonyms StrongDual ùïú E or WeakDual ùïú E, depending on whether it is viewed as equipped with its usual operator norm topology or the weak-* topology.

    It is shown that the canonical mapping StrongDual ùïú E ‚Üí WeakDual ùïú E is continuous, and as a consequence the weak-* topology is coarser than the topology obtained from the operator norm (dual norm).

    In this file, we also establish the Banach-Alaoglu theorem about the compactness of closed balls in the dual of E (as well as sets of somewhat more general form) with respect to the weak-* topology.

    Main definitions #

    The main definitions concern the canonical mapping StrongDual ùïú E ‚Üí WeakDual ùïú E.

    • StrongDual.toWeakDual and WeakDual.toStrongDual: Linear equivalences from StrongDual ùïú E to WeakDual ùïú E and in the converse direction.
    • NormedSpace.Dual.continuousLinearMapToWeakDual: A continuous linear mapping from StrongDual ùïú E to WeakDual ùïú E (same as StrongDual.toWeakDual but different bundled data).

    Main results #

    The first main result concerns the comparison of the operator norm topology on StrongDual ùïú E and the weak-* topology on (its type synonym) WeakDual ùïú E:

    • dual_norm_topology_le_weak_dual_topology: The weak-* topology on the dual of a normed space is coarser (not necessarily strictly) than the operator norm topology.
    • WeakDual.isCompact_polar (a version of the Banach-Alaoglu theorem): The polar set of a neighborhood of the origin in a normed space E over ùïú is compact in WeakDual _ E, if the nontrivially normed field ùïú is proper as a topological space.
    • WeakDual.isCompact_closedBall (the most common special case of the Banach-Alaoglu theorem): Closed balls in the dual of a normed space E over ‚Ñù or ‚ÑÇ are compact in the weak-star topology.

    Implementation notes #

    Weak-* topology is defined generally in the file Mathlib/Topology/Algebra/Module/WeakDual.lean.

    When M is a vector space, the duals StrongDual ùïú M and WeakDual ùïú M are type synonyms with different topology instances.

    For the proof of Banach-Alaoglu theorem, the weak dual of E is embedded in the space of functions E ‚Üí ùïú with the topology of pointwise convergence.

    The polar set polar ùïú s of a subset s of E is originally defined as a subset of the dual StrongDual ùïú E. We care about properties of these w.r.t. weak-* topology, and for this purpose give the definition WeakDual.polar ùïú s for the "same" subset viewed as a subset of WeakDual ùïú E (a type synonym of the dual but with a different topology instance).
