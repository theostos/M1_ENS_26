    Differentiability of monotone functions #

    We show that a monotone function f : ‚Ñù ‚Üí ‚Ñù is differentiable almost everywhere, in Monotone.ae_differentiableAt. (We also give a version for a function monotone on a set, in MonotoneOn.ae_differentiableWithinAt.)

    If the function f is continuous, this follows directly from general differentiation of measure theorems. Let Œº be the Stieltjes measure associated to f. Then, almost everywhere, Œº [x, y] / Leb [x, y] (resp. Œº [y, x] / Leb [y, x]) converges to the Radon-Nikodym derivative of Œº with respect to Lebesgue when y tends to x in (x, +‚àû) (resp.     When f is only monotone, the same argument works up to small adjustments, as the associated Stieltjes measure satisfies Œº [x, y] = f (y^+) - f (x^-) (the right and left limits of f at y and x respectively). One argues that f (x^-) = f x almost everywhere (in fact away from a countable set), and moreover f ((y - (y-x)^2)^+) ‚â§ f y ‚â§ f (y^+). This is enough to deduce the limit of (f y - f x) / (y - x) by a lower and upper approximation argument from the known behavior of Œº [x, y].
